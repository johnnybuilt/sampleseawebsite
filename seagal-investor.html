The Problem

71% of Earth
is Dark

Radio waves — the backbone of WiFi, 5G, and satellite — don't work underwater. They die within centimeters of the surface. The oceans, which cover 71% of the Earth, have no reliable, high-speed wireless communication layer.

Today's underwater systems use cables (expensive, fragile) or low-rate acoustic links (think dial-up modem speeds). Meanwhile, the demand to monitor, operate, and connect underwater infrastructure is exploding.

Sea-Gal Technologies solves this by using sound waves the way WiFi uses radio — but smarter, faster, and with breakthrough MIMO antenna technology adapted from 5G.

Core Technology

How It Works,
Simply Put

01 🔊
Sound as Signal
Just like WiFi sends data on invisible radio waves, our system sends data on precisely engineered sound waves (acoustics). Sound travels well underwater — up to 1,500 m/s. We exploit this to transmit data.
Acoustic Channel
02 📡
MIMO: More Lanes, More Speed
Multiple-Input Multiple-Output (MIMO) is the same technology that powers your 5G phone. Multiple transmitters and receivers work in parallel, multiplying throughput. We're the first to commercialize MIMO for underwater acoustics at this data rate.
MIMO Architecture
03 🧠
AI-Enhanced Signal Processing
The ocean is noisy and unpredictable. Our software uses machine learning to filter noise, compensate for motion, and maintain stable links in real-world conditions — not just lab tanks.
Adaptive ML
04 🔌
Drop-In Hardware
Our modems and transducer components are designed to integrate with existing underwater platforms — ROVs, AUVs, sensors, buoys — without requiring custom redesigns.
Modular Hardware
05 📸
Acoustic Imaging
Our system doubles as an underwater imaging tool — capable of detecting biofouling on ship hulls, structural defects in pipelines, and marine life monitoring, all wirelessly and in real time.
Dual-Use Sensing
06 🌐
Surface Bridge
Data collected underwater is seamlessly relayed to surface gateways and pushed to the cloud or satellite internet — creating a full stack from ocean floor to operator dashboard.
Cloud Integration
